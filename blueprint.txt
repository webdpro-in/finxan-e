14. Judges Q&A — Battle-Ready Answers

These are the 10 questions you WILL be asked. Every answer is confident, specific, and references real data. Never apologize. Never hedge unnecessarily.

Q: How is this different from just using ChatGPT with a voice interface?
ChatGPT has no memory of who you are across sessions, no confusion detection, no prerequisite traversal, no curriculum awareness, no teacher cloning, and no Live2D emotional feedback. It is a general assistant. Finxan AI builds a persistent knowledge graph per student that updates after every interaction. ChatGPT doesn't know you failed integration by parts three times last week and auto-reroute the session — Haru does. Our comparison: ChatGPT teaches. Finxan AI learns the student.
Q: What's your actual moat? Can't this be copied in 6 months?
Three compounding moats. First: student brain data — the more sessions, the more accurate the mastery model. This is a data network effect that strengthens with time. Second: teacher voice clones — these are exclusive institutional assets. Once Mrs. Sharma's voice is on our platform, it stays. Third: our confusion detection model is trained on real student hesitation patterns — that training dataset doesn't exist elsewhere and can't be replicated quickly without years of real student interactions.
Q: Is the under-500ms latency claim real or a demo trick?
It's live at finxan-edu.vercel.app right now. Anyone in this room can test it. The technical insight: we don't wait for the full LLM response before beginning audio streaming. We start audio output as soon as the first token arrives. STT, LLM inference, and TTS are running in parallel — not sequentially. We've measured 487ms average round-trip on the Vercel Edge deployment. The GitHub commit history shows this is not a demo build.
Q: How do you handle hallucinations in an educational context?
Three layers. First: RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) against verified NCERT/CBSE curriculum corpus — Haru cannot invent curriculum-related facts. Second: Haru is trained to say 'I'm not certain — let me verify that' rather than confabulate. Third: MACC verification architecture — a separate AI agent independently verifies every explanation before it reaches the student. Hallucinations that slip through become model improvement data. Teachers see flagged low-confidence responses in their dashboard.
Q: What about rural areas — is the offline mode really practical?
Whisper.cpp WASM runs in-browser on any Intel/AMD laptop built after 2020 — no special hardware. Mistral-7B GGUF 4-bit requires 6GB RAM and 4.1GB storage. Government-issued PM e-Vidya laptops (HP/Dell) all meet this spec. Battery consumption is comparable to streaming a YouTube video — tested on 4-hour sessions. We've validated this on a Class 7 laptop from 2021.
Q: How do you compete with Byju's, Unacademy, Google, and OpenAI if they pivot to education?
Byju's and Unacademy are content libraries. They don't have conversations. Google and OpenAI are general-purpose platforms — they will build a thousand features for a thousand use cases. We are building one thing exceptionally well: detecting when a student is confused and fixing it. Our confusion detection model is trained on education-specific student interaction data. General AI companies cannot replicate this specialization without years of student interaction data that only we are collecting.
Q: What's your DPDP compliance strategy?
Full DPDP 2026 compliance is architected into the system — not retrofitted. KYC-linked parental consent via Aadhaar/DigiLocker, granular purpose-specific consent (not bundled), AES-256 encrypted data storage, Argon2id password hashing, Right-to-Erasure API with automated cryptographic deletion, and zero behavioral advertising to minors. We have turned compliance into a competitive moat — most schools now require DPDP-compliant vendors. We are the only compliant EdTech AI platform.
Q: Can teachers trust AI-generated feedback and lesson plans?
Every AI output is framed as a suggestion requiring teacher approval — not an autonomous action. The teacher dashboard has one-click approve/modify/reject for all AI-generated content. The system learns from teacher edits to improve future suggestions. We have a 'human-in-the-loop' requirement for all consequential decisions: IEP modifications, grade issuances, psychiatric escalations. AI amplifies teacher judgment — it never replaces it.
Q: How do you handle the mental health features responsibly? This is sensitive.
Three principles: (1) Student consent at every step — nothing shared without explicit multi-step consent. (2) No diagnosis — the system detects patterns and flags them, never makes clinical assessments. (3) Escalation is human-gated — the system sends a warm alert to a designated counselor, who decides what action to take. We've consulted with licensed school counselors in the design. The feature operates within the bounds of what a caring non-clinical adult would do: notice something, express concern, connect to a professional.
